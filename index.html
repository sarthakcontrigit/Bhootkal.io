Shaniwar Wada

What is it?

Shaniwar Wada is a 13-story palace fort in Pune. It served as the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818.

Why was it built?

It was built to demonstrate the power of the Peshwas and serve as the capital residence. It was the center of Indian politics in the 18th century.

How was it built?

The foundation was laid in 1730 AD using teak, lime, and stone. Though a massive fire in 1828 destroyed the wooden interiors, the heavy stone fortification walls remain intact.

Sinhagad Fort

What is it?

Sinhagad (The Lion's Fort) is a hill fortress located southwest of Pune. It was previously known as Kondhana.

Why was it important?

It was a strategic military outpost due to its steep natural protection. It is famous for the Battle of Sinhagad (1670), where Tanaji Malusare fought bravely to recapture it.

How was it built?

The fort is built atop a steep cliff in the Bhuleshwar range of the Sahyadris. Its walls were constructed to blend with the natural rock escarpments, making it nearly impossible to breach.

Ajanta Caves

What is it?

The Ajanta Caves are approximately 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments in the Aurangabad district, dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE.

Why was it created?

They served as retreats for Buddhist monks during the monsoon season and as resting places for merchants and pilgrims. They are worship halls and monasteries.

How was it made?

They were carved directly into a horseshoe-shaped cliff of basalt rock. Artisans chiseled the rock from top to bottom to create intricate sculptures and columns without adding any external material.
